# Bimbel OS — Project Vision

## Who This Is For

Bimbel OS exists for the owner-operator of a small, independent tutoring center — typically one owner, one admin, and a handful of teachers, serving somewhere between a few dozen and a couple hundred students. This is not a franchise head office, not a large education company with a dedicated IT team, and not a solo private tutor working alone. It's the owner who is simultaneously doing sales, finance, HR, and operations, and the admin who is holding the entire day-to-day business together across WhatsApp chats and spreadsheets.

## The Problem We Exist to Solve

Right now, the business runs on the admin's memory and manual reconciliation — enrollment, scheduling, attendance, and payments are tracked across disconnected tools with no single source of truth. This isn't a convenience problem; it's a growth ceiling. Every new student adds coordination overhead that scales faster than revenue. The admin's time goes to repetitive data entry and chasing payments instead of supporting teachers and parents. The owner cannot answer basic questions about the health of their own business — active students, outstanding revenue, under-enrolled classes — without hours of manual compilation. Bimbel OS exists to remove that ceiling: to give small tutoring centers the operational backbone that lets them grow without the owner and admin becoming the bottleneck.

## What We Will Deliberately Not Become

- **Not a learning platform.** We are not building curriculum, content delivery, video lessons, or student-facing learning tools. Teaching stays with the teachers.
- **Not a marketplace.** We are not connecting parents to tutors or centers to each other. We serve one center's internal operations, not discovery or matchmaking.
- **Not an enterprise system.** We are not chasing large chains or multi-branch franchises with complex org structures, approval hierarchies, or franchise reporting needs. Optimizing for a 150-branch chain would compromise the simplicity a 1-branch owner needs.
- **Not a replacement for the owner's relationships.** We are not trying to automate away the trust between a center and its families — WhatsApp-style personal communication with parents is a feature of this business, not a bug to be engineered out.
- **Not a general-purpose business tool.** We are not accounting software, not a generic CRM, not a scheduling tool for other industries wearing a tutoring skin.

## What Success Looks Like in Three Years

The owner of a 100-student tutoring center can tell you, in under a minute and without asking their admin, how many active students they have, how much revenue is confirmed versus outstanding, and which classes need attention — because that information is simply available, not assembled. The admin's week is no longer dominated by manually reconciling payment screenshots and retyping the same student information across five places. Centers that were capped at what one overworked admin could manually track are now able to double in size without doubling administrative headcount. And critically, success is measured not in how much software gets used, but in how much less time the owner and admin spend on coordination — and how much more time goes to teaching, parent relationships, and growth.
